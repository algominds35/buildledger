# ReconcileBook — Demo playbook

**Positioning:** You’re selling **time replacement**, not features. Clarity > complexity. Speed > depth.

---

## Before the call (30 min setup)

### 1. Use one clean QuickBooks Online sandbox (or test company)

Create **4 jobs** (customers / sub-customers) with **different stories** so the “aha” is obvious:

| Job name              | Story (what you want on screen) |
|-----------------------|----------------------------------|
| Job A — Mid-progress  | Has contract, costs, **partial** billing → shows **% complete**, **over-billing** OR **under-billing** clearly |
| Job B — Mid-progress  | Opposite billing position vs Job A (if A is over-billed, B is under-billed) |
| Job C — Tight / risk   | Costs near or over budget → triggers **red “over budget”** on job costing |
| Job D — (optional)     | Retainage line visible on WIP if your data supports it |

**Minimum viable demo data**

- Each job: **contract** (invoices) + **costs** (bills/expenses) + **some billing** (invoices progress).
- Aim for **mid-progress**: not 0% and not 100% — so **% complete** and **cost to complete** look real.
- Intentionally create **one over-billed** and **one under-billed** situation on WIP (revenue earned vs billed).
- On job costing, ensure **at least one** job is clearly **over budget** so the **red alert** fires.

### 2. Pre-flight 10 minutes before

- [ ] Log in, open **Dashboard → client → WIP → Job Costing** once (fix any reconnect).
- [ ] Full-screen browser, **one tab**, hide bookmarks bar if possible.
- [ ] **Do not** start on login or pricing — land on **dashboard already logged in**.

## Demo flow (12–15 minutes max)

### 1) Dashboard (60–90 sec)

**Say:**  
“You’re usually juggling each contractor client in its own Excel file or tabs. Here, **every connected client is in one place** — live from QuickBooks.”

**Point at:** client list / portfolio totals (whatever your dashboard highlights).

**Don’t:** deep-dive settings or pricing.

---

### 2) Open one client (30 sec)

**Say:**  
“Normally everything for this client is scattered — WIP in one sheet, job costs in another. Here it’s **one workspace** tied to their books.”

**Click:** into that client (not settings).

---

### 3) WIP — **hero moment #1** (3–4 min) — **PAUSE HERE**

**Say (verbatim-ish):**  
“This whole table — **cost-to-cost % complete**, revenue earned vs billed, over- and under-billing, retainage — is what bookkeepers **rebuild manually every month**. We **generate it from QBO**.”

**Point directly at (slowly):**

1. **% complete** — “This is the cost-to-cost math; doing this in Excel is where the hours go.”
2. **Over billing / Under billing** — “**This number** is what you’re reconciling for hours in spreadsheets.”
3. **Cost to complete** (if visible) — “Same story — usually buried in formulas.”
4. **Retainage** (if you have it) — “Easy to miss when it’s manual.”

**Pause.** Let them read one row. Silence is OK.

**Say:**  
“This alone is often **2–3 hours per client per month** we’re giving back.”

---

### 4) Job costing — **hero moment #2** (2–3 min)

**Say:**  
“This is **where money is going on each job** — and where you’d normally **dig through bills** to see who’s over budget.”

**Point at:**

- The **red alert**: “These jobs are over budget — **you’d have to dig for this**; we **surface it immediately**.”
- One **Over** row vs one **Under** row — “You can see variance **per project** without building the sheet.”

**Say:**  
“If you’re presenting to an owner or a bonding agent, you’re not rebuilding this from scratch the night before.”

---

### 5) PDF (optional, 60 sec)

**Say:**  
“When they ask for a package, you’re not pasting screenshots — **one export**.”

Click **Download PDF** once, show the file name / clean output.

---

### 6) Close (30 sec)

**Ask:** “How many contractor clients are you carrying today?”

**Tie it to time:** “So that’s **X × a few hours a month** on WIP alone — before job-cost fire drills.”

**CTA:** “We do a **14-day trial**, no card — if you want, connect your sandbox or a read-only client and we’ll run this again on your numbers.”

---

## Phrases to steal (emotional, not technical)

- “This number is what you’re **spending hours calculating in Excel**.”
- “This entire WIP table is what people **build manually every month**.”
- “You’d normally **dig for this** — we **surface it instantly**.”
- “This isn’t ‘another report’ — it’s **the report** everyone asks for in construction.”

---

## What to avoid

- Long OAuth / “how the integration works” unless they ask.
- Every column on WIP — only **% complete, over/under billing, cost to complete, retainage**.
- Apologizing for sample data — **never** say “our demo data is messy.”

---

## If numbers look “weird” on screen

- **Scroll** — totals are for **all jobs**, not only what fits on screen.
- **Landscape** PDF if the table looks squeezed in print preview.

---

## One-line product story

**“We replace the monthly WIP and job-cost spreadsheet grind with live QuickBooks reports you can export in seconds.”**

Use that in intros and emails.
